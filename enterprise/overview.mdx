NodePad Enterprise is for teams that want the NodePad workflow inside their own environment. You deploy the application stack, bring the backing services it needs, and control how provider access is configured.

Enterprise is separate from the hosted beta. The beta runs on NodePad-managed infrastructure. Enterprise runs in yours.

## What ships today

- **Self-hosting.** Deploy on Kubernetes with Helm or on a single VM with Docker Compose.
- **Offline / air-gapped install.** Install from an offline bundle when the target environment has no internet access.
- **Immutable release tags.** Releases ship as versioned artifacts so upgrades are deliberate and pin-able.
- **Bring your own provider keys.** Provider access is under your control rather than tied to a shared hosted setup.

## What to plan for

An enterprise deployment is not only "run these containers." Plan for PostgreSQL 15+, Redis 6+, S3-compatible object storage, provider credentials and egress policy, your upgrade and rollback process, and your identity, access, and compliance requirements.

## Who this is for

Enterprise is a fit when you want the NodePad product shape but need deployment control. That usually includes internal infrastructure ownership, controlled provider access, offline or restricted-network installation paths, and explicit change management around upgrades.
