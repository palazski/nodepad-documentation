---
title: "The canvas"
description: "The NodePad canvas is the workspace where branches, references, notes, attachments, and merges stay visible together."
---

The canvas is the main surface inside a NodePad workspace. It holds your conversation branches, text notes, and cross-branch connections in one view so you can see the structure of the work, not only the latest reply.

## Why it matters

Most AI interfaces flatten everything into one timeline. That becomes hard to manage when:

- one question splits into several sub-questions
- you want a second opinion from another model
- a fact-check should stay separate from the main draft
- you need one reply to draw from messages that happened elsewhere

The canvas fixes that by making space a first-class part of the workflow.

## What you can place on it

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Conversation branches" icon="messages-square">
    Each branch is a sequence of user and assistant messages. Branches can continue, split, and reconnect through merge flows.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Text notes" icon="sticky-note">
    Text notes are canvas annotations for you and your team. They are not model instructions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Reference and merge edges" icon="git-merge">
    Edges show how messages relate across branches. They make provenance visible instead of hiding it in prompt text.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Attachments" icon="paperclip">
    Image and file attachments stay attached to the messages that used them and can carry forward into branches and merged drafts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Common canvas actions

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pan and zoom">
    Move around the canvas to inspect one branch closely or zoom out to understand the whole workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search messages">
    Use workspace search to jump between matching messages without losing your spatial layout.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the command palette">
    Open the palette to jump to actions such as merge composition or the reference picker.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Organize the sidebar">
    Favorite workspaces, add tags, duplicate a workspace, or export it when you want a checkpoint.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Think in workspaces, not chats

Each canvas belongs to a workspace. A workspace keeps its own:

- title
- default model
- reasoning preference
- workspace instructions
- branch layout
- tags and favorite state

<Note>
  A good mental model is "one workspace per problem space." Keep one for a feature investigation, another for a long research project, and another for a piece of writing.
</Note>
