NodePad is a spatial workspace for AI conversations. Instead of trapping everything in one scroll, it lets you spread work across a canvas, branch from any point, compare models side by side, and pull useful context back into the next message on purpose.

## What makes NodePad different

Most AI tools force every idea through one thread. NodePad gives you structure instead:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start on a canvas">
    A workspace opens as a blank surface. You can place conversations, notes, and branches where they make sense instead of stacking everything into one timeline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Branch when a thread splits">
    Use **Branch from here** to test a new direction without overwriting the original line of thinking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pull context together deliberately">
    Use `@` references, merge sources, and attachments when you want one message to draw from work that happened elsewhere on the canvas.
  </Step>
</Steps>
