NodePad can use provider keys that belong to you or your organization. That is useful when you want usage, limits, and billing to stay attached to your own provider account.

## Why teams do this

## Where keys live

- In the hosted product, a user can add keys through settings when that feature is enabled.
- In self-hosted environments, operators often configure provider access at the deployment level.

  items={[

<Callout tone="info">
  The exact providers available depend on your NodePad environment. The UI lists only the providers that environment knows how to use.
</Callout>

## When you do not need BYOK

If your current NodePad environment already exposes the models you need, you may not need to add anything. BYOK matters most when you want control over billing, throughput, compliance posture, or model availability.

<Callout tone="note">
  Removing a key can change what appears in the model picker. If a workspace depends on that provider, update the default model after the key change.
</Callout>
