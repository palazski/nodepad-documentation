---
title: "Bring your own Anthropic and OpenAI API keys"
description: "Connect your own Anthropic or OpenAI API key so NodePad routes model requests through your account, with your pricing and your rate limits."
---

NodePad supports bringing your own API keys for Anthropic and OpenAI. When you connect a provider key in settings, NodePad routes that provider's requests through your account — so usage bills against your contract with the provider, not NodePad's retail rates, and prompts/completions flow through infrastructure you already trust.

## Why bring your own keys

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Use your negotiated pricing" icon="tag">
    If your organization has volume pricing with Anthropic or OpenAI, your NodePad usage bills against those rates.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Your rate limits, not ours" icon="gauge">
    Heavy canvas sessions don't share a pool with other NodePad users. You inherit whatever rate limits your provider account has.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Audit trail in one place" icon="clipboard-list">
    Provider-side usage dashboards show exactly what NodePad used on your behalf.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Per-user or shared keys" icon="users">
    Use your personal provider keys, or configure a workspace-level key that everyone in your account inherits.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How it works

Keys are stored encrypted at rest and only decrypted in memory when NodePad dispatches a model request. They're never displayed back to you after you save them, and admins can see which providers are connected without seeing the key values themselves.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    From any canvas, open the Settings panel using the menu in the top-right corner of the NodePad interface.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Providers">
    Select the **Providers** tab. You'll see the providers NodePad supports and any keys currently connected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your key">
    Paste your Anthropic or OpenAI API key. Save.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the new models">
    Go back to your canvas. Models that are only reachable with your key will appear alongside NodePad's shared-tier models in the model picker.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  You can remove a key at any time from the Providers tab. After removal, NodePad falls back to the shared-tier models for that provider (or to no models from that provider, on Enterprise installs that don't ship shared keys).
</Info>

## What you don't need keys for

You don't have to bring your own keys to use NodePad on the beta — the shared-tier models work out of the box. BYOK is for teams that want to tie spend and traffic to their own provider accounts. On self-hosted Enterprise installs, the deployment operator typically configures provider keys at the system level so every user can chat without each one pasting a key in settings.
